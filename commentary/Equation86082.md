## The law for modules over the Gaussian integers

As [we discuss here](https://leanprover.zulipchat.com/#narrow/channel/458659-Equational/topic/The.20.22modules.20over.20Gaussian.20integers.22.20law/with/519928430), this order-6 law characterizes the operation `x ◇ y = x + i y` in modules over the ring `ℤ[i]` of Gaussian integers, where `i` is the square root of -1.

The equivalence class of this law among laws of order 6 has 18 to 25 members: the laws 86082, [86160](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?86160), [125935](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?125935), [127386](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?127386), [127464](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?127464), [127560](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?127560), [128214](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?128214), [184602](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?184602), [185406](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?185406), [185442](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?185442), [186339](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?186339), [251586](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?251586), [251959](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?251959), [252414](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?252414), [252439](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?252439), [444561](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?444561), [446262](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?446262), [446340](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?446340) are equivalent; the laws [86046](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?86046), [86064](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?86064), [444507](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?444507), [446226](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?446226), [446244](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?446244) are only known to be equivalent in the finite case and their equivalence in the infinite case is unknown; and the remaining candidates are [127759](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?127759), [183687](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?183687).

See also the [Tarski law 543](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?543) for modules over ℤ (namely abelian groups), [law 895](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?895) for modules over ℤ/2ℤ, and [law 85914](https://teorth.github.io/equational_theories/implications/?85914) for modules over Eisenstein integers.
